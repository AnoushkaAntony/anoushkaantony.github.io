Active Projects
Ashoka University Aug 2025 – Present Undergraduate Thesis

Future Self-Continuity and Risk-Taking among Indian Young Adults

How does the strength of our connection to our future selves shape the risks we take today? This two-study mixed-methods thesis (N≈600) investigates the causal role of affective-identity connection to the future self on risk-taking behaviour.

Study 1 (Experimental): Tests the causal role of future self-continuity on 'hot' vs. 'cold' decision-making using a letter-writing intervention and the Columbia Card Task.

Study 2 (Correlational): Analyses a moderated mediation model to determine whether Consideration of Future Consequences and Temporal Discounting mediate risk behaviours across five domains.

Mixed Methods Experimental Correlational N=600 Decision-Making Identity

Supervisor: Prof. Naseer Ahmad Bhat, Department of Psychology, Ashoka University

Duke University Global Health Study · Udayan Care May – Jul 2025 Field Research Internship

Mental Health and Caregiving Models for Orphaned and Separated Children in India

Qualitative field research examining mental health, resilience, and caregiving structures for children in institutional care across Delhi. Conducted in-depth interviews using trauma-informed and culturally sensitive protocols, translated psychometric instruments between English and Hindi, and facilitated workshops on coping strategies, art expression, and career readiness for youth in care homes.

Maintained strict ethical standards across all participant interactions and data management processes, with particular attention to the vulnerabilities of the study population.
