Philosophy

How I Think About Teaching

The classroom — whether a lecture hall or a discussion group of eight — is a space where ideas should feel alive. I design sessions that ask students to do something with knowledge, not just receive it.

Psychology, in particular, lends itself to this. Almost every concept has a lived dimension. When students can connect a theory to an experience — their own, or someone they know — it stops being abstract.

I also think good teaching is honest about uncertainty. Saying "we don't fully know yet" is not a failure — it's an invitation to research.

"Understanding — not memorisation — is the goal."
